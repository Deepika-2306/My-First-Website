Recipe 1: Panna Cotta with blueberry compote



by Deepika chowdary

Panna cotta (Italian for "cooked cream" as panna means cream and cotta means cooking) is an Italian dessert of sweetened cream thickened with gelatin and molded. The name panna cotta is not mentioned in Italian cookbooks before the 1960s, yet it is often cited as a traditional dessert of the northern Italian region of Piedmont.

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 5 minutes
  • Total time: 20 minutes + Cooling time
  • yeild: 6-8 servings

Materials:

Ingrediants Volume
Whole Milk 200ml
Fresh Cream 200ml
Gelatine crystals 1 tablespoon
Sugar 0.25 cup
Vanilla extract 2 teaspoons

Method:

  1. Take a bowl and mix gelatin crystals and water together.
  2. Heat some water in a saucepan. Once the water is hot, turn off the stove and place the gelatin bowl in the hot water. Let the gelatin melt completely from the external heat.
  3. Take a small saucepan. Add milk and fresh cream to the pan and mix it well. Keep the flame on a medium-low.
  4. Add sugar, vanilla extract and melted gelatin to the pan. Turn off the stove and mix all the ingredients together.
  5. Take some small bowls (You can also use small glasses or cups) and pour the mixture into that.
  6. Cling wrap the bowls and refrigerate it. (Never keep it in a freezer)
  7. Once refrigerated, keep the bowl in hot water so that the panna cotta will get loosened from the sides. Gently use a knife to release the panna cotta from the bowl.
  8. Place the plate upside down on the bowl. Panna cotta will come off.
  9. Put blueberry compote and garnish it with some fresh mint leaves or moringa leaves(as shown).
    10. Yummy Panna cotta is ready to be served!
